Safe & Equitable Return

I believe in returning kids to school only when it is safe, while ensuring that those whose educational needs are not served by distance learning still receive a quality education.

Distance learning is an option that we should strive to improve, so that parents who feel their kids can learn effectively in that fashion can take that option. It reduces the number of kids in the building, making it safer for everyone. For parents whose kids require in-person learning, we should have plans in place to ensure they are distanced, masked, and in a stable group.

Parents should be made aware of the level of risk the school is assuming, based on daily metrics from the Rhode Island Department of Health. Plans should be communicated with ample time for parents to digest and make their choices, with knowledge of what happens in the event of an infection, either student or teacher.

Budget Transparency

We should all be able to see where our money is going in the budget. A transparent, accessible budgeting process is key to discussing our priorities in our education dollars.

Working with spreadsheets and churning through numbers is what I love to do. I will bring a keen eye and an analytical mind to our budgeting process. I want to know where our money is going, and how we can get the most out of the dollars that are available.

We should all have a clear sense of how those dollars are allocated, so that we can have a fair discussion of what our priorities are. I will work to make that happen.
